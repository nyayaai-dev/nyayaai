Privacy Policy

Last updated 15 July 2026 · Effective for all use of nyayaai.example and its AI consultation service.

NyayaAI is built around a simple principle: you shouldn't have to hand over personal information just to ask a legal question. This policy explains, plainly, what we actually collect, why, and what rights you have over it under Indian law — principally the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act").

In short

🔎

We don't require an account to use the AI consultation. We don't ask for your name, phone number, or government ID to answer a legal question. We don't sell data — there's no advertising business here to sell it to. The main things we do store are technical (like your one-time acknowledgement of the site disclaimer) and, if you write to us, whatever you choose to put in that message.

1. Who this policy covers

This policy applies to NyayaAI (the "Platform", "we", "us"), an AI-operated legal information website covering Indian law, and to anyone who visits or uses it (the "user", "you"). NyayaAI is not a law firm and does not maintain client files, case records, or a client intake system — the data footprint here is intentionally small.

2. What we collect

a. Information you choose to give us

When you use the AI Consultation chat, the text of your questions is processed in order to generate a response. When you use the Contact page, whatever you type into that form (name, email, and message) is sent — via your own email client, as a normal email — to our inbox; we don't capture it into a separate database first. We ask you, in both places, not to include sensitive identifiers such as your Aadhaar number, PAN, passwords, or financial account details, because the service simply has no need for them.

b. Information collected automatically

Like most websites, standard web server logs (IP address, browser type, page requested, timestamp) may be generated by the hosting infrastructure for security and reliability purposes. We use your browser's local storage (not cookies) for exactly two small flags: whether you've acknowledged the entry disclaimer, and — if you switch to a live AI backend rather than demo mode — nothing beyond what's needed to keep a conversation coherent while your tab is open.

c. Information we do not collect

We do not require registration, so we do not collect account credentials. We do not run advertising trackers, third-party analytics pixels, or cross-site tracking cookies. We do not ask for or store government identity numbers, financial information, or biometric data under any normal use of the Platform.

4. How the AI consultation processes your questions

By default, NyayaAI runs in a local "demo mode" — your question is matched against a static knowledge base entirely inside your own browser, and nothing is transmitted anywhere. If a live AI backend has been configured (see the project's technical documentation), your message is sent to that backend, which in turn may call a third-party language-model provider (for example, Anthropic or OpenAI) to generate a reply. In that case, your message is subject to that provider's own data-handling terms in addition to this policy, and is not stored by NyayaAI itself beyond what's needed to keep the current conversation in context.

5. Your rights as a Data Principal

Under the DPDP Act, 2023, you — as a "Data Principal" — have the right to:

  • Access a summary of the personal data we hold about you and how it's being processed.
  • Correction and erasure of personal data you've given us, where it's inaccurate, incomplete, or no longer needed for the purpose it was given.
  • Withdraw consent at any time for processing based on your consent (this won't affect processing already carried out).
  • Grievance redressal — you can raise a concern with us directly (see below) before approaching the Data Protection Board of India.
  • Nominate another individual to exercise these rights on your behalf in the event of death or incapacity.

Because NyayaAI collects so little personal data by design, most of these requests can typically be resolved simply — for most users, there is nothing beyond a disclaimer-acknowledgement flag on their own device to access, correct, or erase.

6. How long we keep data

Chat messages processed in demo mode are never transmitted or stored server-side at all — they exist only in your browser tab. Where a live AI backend is in use, messages are retained only for the duration needed to maintain conversational context, and are not persisted into a long-term database by NyayaAI. Contact-form emails are kept for as long as reasonably needed to address the matter raised, then deleted or archived per standard email retention practice.

7. Cookies & local storage

NyayaAI does not use tracking cookies. It uses your browser's localStorage — a standard web technology, not a cookie — to remember that you've dismissed the entry disclaimer, purely so you're not shown it on every page load. You can clear this at any time via your browser's site-data settings, which will simply cause the notice to reappear.

8. Third-party sharing

We share data with third parties only where necessary to operate the service: a live AI backend/LLM provider (only if and when one is configured — see §4), and our hosting/infrastructure provider, which processes standard server logs in the ordinary course of serving the website. We do not share data with data brokers, advertisers, or for any purpose unrelated to operating NyayaAI itself.

9. Children's privacy

Legal questions are relevant to people of all ages, and we don't operate an age gate. However, in line with the DPDP Act's requirements around processing children's data, we do not knowingly collect additional personal data, run targeted profiling, or require identity verification from users who identify as minors. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child has shared personal information with us beyond a general legal question, contact us and we'll remove it.

10. Security

We apply reasonable technical safeguards appropriate to the limited data we hold — encrypted transport (HTTPS), and avoiding unnecessary data collection in the first place, which remains the most effective safeguard of all. No system is completely immune to compromise, and we can't guarantee absolute security, but we also aren't sitting on a large database of sensitive data that would make us an attractive target.

11. If something goes wrong

In the event of a personal data breach that's likely to affect you, we will notify affected users and the Data Protection Board of India as required under the DPDP Act, without undue delay, along with the steps we're taking in response.

12. Grievance Officer & Data Protection contact

In accordance with the DPDP Act, 2023 and the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021, questions, complaints, or requests relating to your personal data can be sent to our Grievance Officer:

Grievance Officer / Data Protection contact

Email: privacy@nyayaai.example

We aim to acknowledge complaints within 24 hours and resolve them within 15 days, in line with the IT Rules, 2021. This role is currently staffed by the small team that maintains NyayaAI directly — see the About page.

If you're not satisfied with our response, you may escalate a personal-data complaint to the Data Protection Board of India once it is operational for grievance intake in your matter.

13. Changes to this policy

We'll update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page whenever this policy changes, and for material changes (for example, if we ever begin sharing data more broadly), we'll flag it prominently on the site rather than relying on a quiet date change alone.

14. Contact

For anything not covered above, reach us through the Contact page.
