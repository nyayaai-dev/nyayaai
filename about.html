About NyayaAI

Legal knowledge, without the gatekeeping

We built NyayaAI after noticing the same pattern over and over: someone has a legal question that's probably simple to answer, but they sit on it for weeks because they don't know a lawyer, can't justify a consultation fee for "just a question," or feel a little silly asking. That gap — between having a question and knowing where to take it — is the whole reason this exists.

Who's actually behind this

The conversation itself is 100% AI, start to finish — no one is reading your chats or standing between you and an answer. But NyayaAI isn't unattended: a small team of engineers and legal researchers builds and maintains the knowledge base behind it, cross-checks it against the official Gazette text, and updates it as amendments, new codes, and judgments come in. We'd rather be upfront about that split — AI-operated conversations, human-maintained accuracy — than let either half of it be a surprise.

🤖

Purely AI-operated

No call centre, no paralegal queue, no human reviewer sitting between you and an answer. Every response is generated by the AI assistant directly from the statute and case-law knowledge base — instantly, at any hour.

📚

Grounded in cited law

Answers reference the specific Article, Section, or Act — the Constitution, BNS/BNSS/BSA, Companies Act, Labour Codes, DPDP Act and more — instead of vague generalities, so you can verify every claim yourself.

🕰️

Tracks current affairs

India's laws changed enormously in the last two years — new criminal codes, four Labour Codes, a new Income Tax Act, a data-protection regime. The Current Affairs section tracks how Parliament and the courts are actively reshaping the law.

⚠️

Knows its limits

NyayaAI cannot file a case, appear before a court or tribunal, notarise a document, or give advice tailored to facts only a human interview would surface. For those, it will tell you plainly to consult a licensed advocate.

How the AI consultation actually works

When you ask a question, NyayaAI matches it against a structured knowledge base of current Indian statutes and recent judgments, then composes a plain-language answer with citations back to the exact provision — the same process a junior associate would follow when researching a client query, minus the wait.

🧪

Current build status: the assistant runs in a local knowledge-base "demo mode" until a live LLM backend is connected (see the project README). In demo mode it only draws on the seeded practice-area content on this site, not the open internet — so treat it as a research starting point, not a final answer, and always verify citations against the official bare act, especially given courts have begun flagging AI-hallucinated case law (Supreme Court, July 2026).
