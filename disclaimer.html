Legal Disclaimer

Last updated 15 July 2026. Read alongside the Terms of Use and Privacy Policy.

⚠️

The short version: NyayaAI gives you legal information, cited to the actual statute or judgment, so you understand your situation before deciding what to do next. It is not a law firm, does not replace a licensed advocate, and cannot act on your behalf. If real money, liberty, or a signature is on the line, verify what you read here and speak to an advocate.

1. NyayaAI is not a law firm

NyayaAI is an artificial-intelligence information system. It is not a law firm, does not employ or supervise any advocate, and no output from this Platform constitutes legal advice, a legal opinion, or solicitation of legal work. Nothing on this website is intended as advertising or solicitation by a legal practitioner, in line with the restrictions the Bar Council of India places on how legal services may be marketed in India.

2. Information, not advice

Content is generated from a curated knowledge base of Indian statutes, constitutional provisions, and reported judgments, and is provided for general informational and educational purposes only. Legal advice, properly understood, requires a professional to review your specific facts, documents, and evidence, exercise judgment about strategy, and take responsibility for that judgment — none of which an information platform, AI or otherwise, can do. Treat every answer here as a starting point for understanding your situation, not a final determination of it.

3. No advocate-client relationship

Use of this website or its AI chat does not create an advocate-client relationship, legal privilege, or confidentiality obligation of the kind that exists between a client and a licensed advocate under the Advocates Act, 1961 and the Bar Council of India Rules. Please don't share information in the chat that you would only disclose under legal privilege, and see our Privacy Policy for how your messages are actually handled.

4. Laws change — verify before you rely

Laws change frequently, through amendment, repeal, delegated legislation (rules and notifications), and judicial interpretation. We maintain a "status as of" date on every practice-area page and try to keep pace with major developments — the 2024 criminal-law overhaul, the 2025-26 Labour Code rules, the 2025 Income Tax Act, and so on — but this Platform may not reflect the most recent development at the exact moment you read it. Before relying on any information here for a real decision — filing a case, signing a contract, responding to a notice, or any matter with financial, criminal, or personal-liberty consequences — independently verify the relevant provision against the official Gazette text.

5. The limits of an AI system specifically

Beyond the general limits of any legal-information resource, an AI system carries its own risks: it can misread a nuance in your question, apply a provision that's since been amended, or — in the worst case — generate a plausible-sounding but incorrect answer. This isn't a hypothetical concern in Indian courts either: in July 2026, the Supreme Court directly addressed the problem of AI-generated, hallucinated case citations being relied upon in judicial filings. We built NyayaAI to cite its sources precisely so that you (or an advocate) can check them — please actually do that checking, especially for anything that surprises you.

6. What NyayaAI cannot do

NyayaAI does not, and cannot:

  • Appear in court or before any tribunal, regulator, or authority on your behalf;
  • Draft, sign, or file pleadings, petitions, or statutory notices for you;
  • Notarise, attest, or witness any document;
  • Guarantee the outcome of any dispute, negotiation, or application;
  • Give advice that accounts for facts, evidence, or strategy specific to your matter beyond what you've described in the chat.

For all of the above, you need a licensed advocate enrolled with a State Bar Council — NyayaAI can help you understand what you're walking into when you engage one.

7. Accuracy of Current Affairs & Insights content

The Current Affairs section is a curated, periodically-updated feed, not a real-time newswire — always check the original source (linked on each item) and the official Parliament, Supreme Court, or Gazette record before treating any item as authoritative. The step-by-step guides in Insights describe the general process as commonly followed; specific offices, forms, or timelines can vary by state and change over time.
